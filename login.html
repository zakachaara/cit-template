What is IDEH ?͏͏ 

International Days of Ethical Hacking (IDEH)
Since 2019, the CIT has hosted the annual International Days of Ethical Hacking (IDEH), an event dedicated to exploring the latest trends in information system security.
IDEH serves as a platform for knowledge exchange, bringing together cybersecurity enthusiasts and industry experts to discuss a unique and timely theme each year. The event features a dynamic mix of conferences and hands-on workshops, fostering collaboration and learning. To cap off the experience, participants can join an exhilarating Capture The Flag (CTF) competition, where aspiring cybersecurity professionals battle for prestigious prizes and recognition.
@
Open Conferences for All
Open Conferences for All
The IDEH welcomes everyone to its conferences, even those not participating in the competition. This makes IDEH a unique opportunity to unite cybersecurity enthusiasts from all backgrounds. The event kicks off with expert-led conferences that delve into various facets of cybersecurity. These sessions cover a wide range of topics, allowing participants to explore new areas within the field and engage directly with renowned speakers. Whether you're a seasoned professional or simply curious about cybersecurity, IDEH offers an enriching experience for all.
#
Hands-On Learning Opportunities
Workshops
Following the expert-led conferences, participants have the chance to dive deeper into cybersecurity through interactive workshops. These workshops are designed to put the knowledge gained during the conferences into practice, bridging the gap between theory and real-world application. Led by experienced professionals, the workshops offer participants a unique opportunity to enhance their skills in a collaborative, hands-on environment. Topics range from ethical hacking techniques to advanced cybersecurity tools and practices, ensuring that both beginners and seasoned professionals find value in the experience.
$
CTF Competition
A Test of Skill and Strategy
The event culminates in the renowned Capture The Flag (CTF) competition, a thrilling opportunity for participants to put their cybersecurity skills to the ultimate test. Spanning 12 intense hours, the competition challenges participants with high-quality tasks across a wide range of cybersecurity categories. Each challenge is meticulously crafted by top experts in the field, ensuring a stimulating and rewarding experience for all skill levels.
  • Duration: 12 hours
  • Official Team: 4 students (must be on-site)
  • Unofficial Teams: Anyone (join on-line)
  • Theme: CyberSecurity and AI
